FAQ: What is NFPA 291 fire flow testing?

Standardized hydrant testing to determine available flow at 20 psi, using static/residual pressure and pitot readings to verify supply and classify hydrants.

FAQ: How much does testing cost in Ontario?

Pricing depends on volume and travel. Programs start from per‑hydrant rates with volume discounts for small municipalities. Request a quote for an exact estimate.

FAQ: What’s included in the report?

PDF report with location details, pressures, calculated available flow at 20 psi, NFPA color class, notes/photos, and optional GIS/CSV exports.

FAQ: How long per hydrant?

Typical tests take 20–40 minutes per hydrant depending on site conditions, traffic control, and coordination needs.
